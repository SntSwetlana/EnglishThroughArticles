When AI builds itself

Level: C1–C2
IELTS Target: 8.0–9.0
Source: The Economist

What happens when artificial intelligence becomes capable of improving itself?

This article explores the idea of recursive self-improvement (RSI) — a process in which AI systems help create increasingly powerful successor models with less and less human involvement.

Could this accelerate scientific progress, transform entire industries, and eventually lead to superintelligence?

Or could humanity lose control of systems it no longer fully understands?

A fascinating and thought-provoking passage on AI, automation, technological progress, and the future of human decision-making.
